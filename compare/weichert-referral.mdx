Weichert Real Estate is one of the most recognized names in New Jersey real estate. Their referral program lets you stay licensed under the Weichert umbrella while earning referral income. However, that brand recognition comes at a significant cost — Weichert's effective commission split means you keep only 40–50% of each referral fee. If you are weighing brand prestige against maximizing your take-home income, this comparison will help you decide which program is the right fit.

## Commission Deep Dive

The commission structure is the single biggest difference between these two programs. Here is how each model works on the same transaction.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="General Referral Model">
    General Referral uses a **flat-fee model**: you keep 100% of your referral fee, minus a flat \$295 transaction fee. The fee stays the same regardless of the referral amount.

    **Worked example — \$500K home sale:**

    | Line Item | Amount |
    |---|---|
    | Home sale price | \$500,000 |
    | Gross commission (3%) | \$15,000 |
    | Your referral fee (25%) | \$3,750 |
    | General Referral transaction fee | -\$295 |
    | **Your take-home** | **\$3,455 (92.1%)** |

    Because the fee is flat, your effective percentage improves as the transaction value increases.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Weichert Model">
    Weichert uses a **percentage-based split**: the brokerage retains approximately 40–50% of each referral fee through their commission structure.

    **Worked example — \$500K home sale:**

    | Line Item | Amount |
    |---|---|
    | Home sale price | \$500,000 |
    | Gross commission (3%) | \$15,000 |
    | Your referral fee (25%) | \$3,750 |
    | Weichert's share (40–50%) | -\$1,500 to -\$1,875 |
    | **Your take-home** | **\$1,875–\$2,250 (50–60%)** |

    The percentage split means the brokerage's share grows proportionally with the transaction value.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Info>
  On this single transaction, the difference is **\$1,205–\$1,580** in favor of General Referral. Over multiple referrals per year, this gap compounds significantly.
</Info>

  </Tab>

## Who Should Choose Weichert?

Weichert's referral program may be a better fit if you:

- Value the Weichert brand name and want to stay affiliated with a recognized NJ brokerage
- Want access to Weichert's training programs and professional development resources
- Prefer working within an established, traditional brokerage structure
- Are less concerned about maximizing your per-referral income and prioritize brand association

<Note>
  Weichert has been a trusted name in New Jersey real estate for decades. If brand recognition and established training programs are your top priorities, their referral program provides that affiliation — though at a higher commission cost.
</Note>

## Who Should Choose General Referral?

General Referral is the better choice if you:

- Want to **maximize your take-home income** on every referral
- Prefer an **online-first experience** with digital tools for submitting and tracking referrals
- Want the freedom to send referrals to **any agent at any brokerage** with no network restrictions
- Value **simplicity and transparency** — one flat fee, no percentage splits, no surprises
- Want to sign up and get started **entirely online** without visiting an office

<Tip>
  With General Referral's flat-fee model, the more valuable the transaction, the higher your effective commission percentage. On a \$500K home, you keep 92.1%. On a \$750K home, you keep 94.8%.
</Tip>
