Coldwell Banker is one of the most recognizable names in real estate, with a national footprint spanning thousands of offices. Their referral program gives agents access to that brand recognition -- but it comes at a steep cost. Coldwell Banker takes a 50% commission split on every referral, meaning half of every check you earn goes back to the brokerage. If you are deciding between General Referral and Coldwell Banker's referral program, the core question is straightforward: is the brand name worth giving up half your income?

## Commission Deep Dive

The difference between a flat fee and a 50% split becomes dramatic as referral values increase. Here is how the two models compare on a typical transaction.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="General Referral Model">
    **Example: \$500K home, 3% commission (\$15,000), 25% referral fee (\$3,750)**

    | Line Item | Amount |
    |---|---|
    | Referral fee earned | \$3,750 |
    | Less: flat transaction fee | -\$295 |
    | **Your take-home** | **\$3,455** |
    | **You keep** | **92.1%** |

    With General Referral, you pay a predictable \$295 flat fee per transaction regardless of the referral value. The higher the transaction price, the more you benefit from the flat-fee model.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Coldwell Banker Model">
    **Example: \$500K home, 3% commission (\$15,000), 25% referral fee (\$3,750)**

    | Line Item | Amount |
    |---|---|
    | Referral fee earned | \$3,750 |
    | Less: 50% brokerage split | -\$1,875 |
    | **Your take-home** | **\$1,875** |
    | **You keep** | **50.0%** |

    With Coldwell Banker, you give up half of every referral fee to the brokerage. On this single transaction, that is **\$1,580 less** than you would keep with General Referral.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  On a single \$500K referral, the Coldwell Banker 50% split costs you \$1,580 more than General Referral's flat \$295 fee. That gap widens with every additional referral you send.
</Warning>

## Who Should Choose Coldwell Banker?

Coldwell Banker's referral program may be the right fit if you:

- **Value the national CB brand above all else** -- Coldwell Banker is one of the most recognized names in real estate, and some agents find that brand association valuable for their personal network
- **Want access to CB's corporate resources and training** -- Coldwell Banker University and their corporate training programs are well-established
- **Are comfortable with a 50% split** -- If brand affiliation matters more to you than maximizing per-referral income, the 50% split may be an acceptable trade-off

<Note>
  Keep in mind that as a referral agent, you are not actively selling under the Coldwell Banker brand -- you are simply passing leads to other agents. The brand value you receive as a referral-only agent is significantly less than what a full-service CB agent gets.
</Note>

## Who Should Choose General Referral?

General Referral is the stronger choice if you:

- **Want to maximize your income** -- A flat \$295 fee keeps far more money in your pocket than a 50% split, especially as referral values grow
- **Prefer online tools and a modern workflow** -- Submit referrals, sign agreements, and track commissions from your computer or phone
- **Want no restrictions on where you send referrals** -- Refer to any licensed agent at any brokerage, anywhere in the country, with no network limitations
