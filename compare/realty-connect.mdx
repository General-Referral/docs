Realty Connect is a national referral brokerage with offices in Reston, Virginia and San Francisco, California. They offer an 80% commission split with a \$100 annual fee, giving agents a straightforward way to earn referral income across state lines. However, Realty Connect has no New Jersey office presence -- which means NJ-licensed agents are working with a brokerage headquartered hundreds of miles away. If local support, NJ market knowledge, and maximizing your per-referral income matter to you, this comparison will help you weigh the trade-offs.

## Commission Deep Dive

The commission structure is the most impactful difference between these two programs. General Referral's flat fee becomes increasingly advantageous as referral values grow, while Realty Connect's 20% cut scales proportionally with every transaction.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="General Referral Model">
    With General Referral, you keep 100% of your referral commission minus a flat \$295 transaction fee. The fee never changes, regardless of the referral amount.

    **Example: \$500K home, 3% commission (\$15,000), 25% referral fee (\$3,750)**

    | Line Item | Amount |
    |---|---|
    | Referral fee earned | \$3,750 |
    | Less: flat transaction fee | -\$295 |
    | **Your take-home** | **\$3,455** |
    | **You keep** | **92.1%** |

    Because the fee is flat, your effective percentage improves as the transaction value increases.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Realty Connect Model">
    With Realty Connect, you receive 80% of your referral commission. The brokerage retains 20% of every referral fee.

    **Example: \$500K home, 3% commission (\$15,000), 25% referral fee (\$3,750)**

    | Line Item | Amount |
    |---|---|
    | Referral fee earned | \$3,750 |
    | Less: 20% brokerage split | -\$750 |
    | **Your take-home** | **\$3,000** |
    | **You keep** | **80.0%** |

    The percentage-based split means the brokerage's share grows proportionally with the referral value.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Info>
  On this single transaction, General Referral puts **\$455 more** in your pocket compared to Realty Connect's 80% split. That \$455 difference repeats on every referral of the same value -- and the gap widens on higher-value transactions.
</Info>

    - **No commission tracking dashboard** -- Status updates are handled through direct communication with the brokerage
  </Tab>

## Who Should Choose Realty Connect?

Realty Connect may be a good fit if you:

- **Are licensed in multiple states** and want a single national brokerage to handle referrals across all of them
- **Do not need local NJ support** and are comfortable working with an out-of-state brokerage for all referral activity
- **Are located near Reston, VA or San Francisco, CA** and want convenient access to a physical office
- **Prefer a national brand** over a brokerage that specializes in a specific market

<Note>
  If you are a New Jersey agent whose primary referral activity is within or connected to the NJ market, Realty Connect's lack of local presence may limit the hands-on support you receive. For agents licensed in multiple states who want one brokerage nationwide, Realty Connect's national model may be appealing.
</Note>

## Who Should Choose General Referral?

General Referral is the stronger choice if you:

- **Are a New Jersey agent** who wants a local broker with physical offices in Midland Park and Holmdel
- **Want to maximize your income** with a flat \$295 transaction fee instead of giving up 20% of every referral
- **Prefer online tools** for submitting referrals, signing agreements, and tracking commissions
- **Want a brokerage that specializes in the NJ market** and understands the local real estate landscape
- **Value transparent, predictable costs** -- one flat fee, no percentage splits, no surprises

<Tip>
  With General Referral's flat-fee model, the more valuable the transaction, the higher your effective commission percentage. On a \$500K home, you keep 92.1%. On a \$750K home, you keep 94.8%.
</Tip>
