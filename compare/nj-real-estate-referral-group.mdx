NJ Real Estate Referral Group (Daunno Realty / NJRERG) offers a 90% commission split, which is among the higher percentage-based splits available in New Jersey. However, that 90% rate only applies to in-network referrals -- referrals sent to agents within NJRERG's own network. This in-network restriction significantly limits your earning potential and flexibility, since you cannot freely choose the best agent for your client without potentially giving up a portion of your commission.

General Referral takes a different approach: you keep 100% of your referral fee, less a flat \$295 transaction fee, with no restrictions on where you send referrals. You choose the agent, the brokerage, and the market -- and your payout stays the same every time.

## Commission Deep Dive

Let's walk through a real scenario so you can see exactly how each model works. We'll use a \$500K home with a 3% commission (\$15,000 gross) and a standard 25% referral fee (\$3,750).

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="General Referral Model">
    **Your referral fee:** \$3,750

    - Flat transaction fee: **\$295**
    - Your take-home: **\$3,750 - \$295 = \$3,455**
    - Effective split: **92.1%**

    You pay the same \$295 regardless of the property value. On higher-value transactions, your effective split climbs even higher.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="NJRERG Model">
    **Your referral fee:** \$3,750

    - Brokerage takes 10%: **\$375**
    - Your take-home: **\$3,750 x 90% = \$3,375**
    - Effective split: **90.0%**

    The 10% brokerage cut scales with the referral fee, so you give up more in dollar terms on higher-value transactions.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Difference: You keep \$80 more per referral with General Referral on a \$500K home.**

<Warning>
  The 90% commission split at NJRERG only applies to in-network referrals -- referrals sent to agents within their network. If you refer to an agent outside of their network, you may receive a lower split. Make sure you understand the terms before signing up.
</Warning>

## Who Should Choose NJ Real Estate Referral Group?

NJRERG may be a good fit if you:

- Primarily refer clients to agents who are already part of NJRERG's network
- Are located near Clark, NJ and prefer a local, single-office brokerage
- Are comfortable with the in-network restriction and do not need flexibility to refer to agents at other brokerages
- Prefer a familiar, traditional referral process over digital tools

If your referral activity fits neatly within their network, the 90% split and \$99 annual fee make NJRERG a reasonable option.

## Who Should Choose General Referral?

General Referral is the stronger choice if you:

- Want no restrictions on where you send referrals -- refer to any licensed agent at any brokerage, anywhere in the country
- Want to keep more per referral, especially on higher-value transactions, thanks to the flat \$295 fee
- Prefer online tools for submitting referrals, signing agreements, and tracking commissions
- Value the flexibility to match your client with the best possible agent, regardless of network affiliation
- Want a straightforward, transparent commission structure with no hidden conditions
